Deadhand Card Game — Original Brief
Captured: 2026-06-15

Engine: Godot (reference setup: ~/projects/throw-fight)
Art: 1800s card art

Pitch:
Dark fantasy. 1800s small Texas town called Deadhand, TX. The mayor is evil —
throwing people in jail for nothing, hanging innocents, terrorizing the town
quietly and sneakily. You start with $2 and need to make money.

Player actions: rob graves, mine for gold, hold up stage coaches, rob banks,
fish, duels, etc.

The player isn't necessarily a good person — but the mayor is worse.

Economy:
- Sell at the local store
- Saloon sells drinks with various buffs / curses
- Buy clothes for bonuses & penalties (e.g. duster intimidates duel opponent
  to fire slower / less accurately)

Card philosophy:
- Everything is card-based
- Cards are simple: numbers and text (like a regular deck)
- Special cards mixed in (encounters, items, like Monopoly Chance cards)
- Hand of mostly number cards + some face cards
- Choose a task and play cards from hand to execute it
- Plays like a mix of MtG + poker/blackjack but simpler than MtG
- Items/cards earned by completing tasks & encounters
- Draw from "items" pile after wins
- Encounter cards (e.g. duels) interrupt or are chosen

Goal: gain enough wealth + items to defeat the mayor.

Tone & inspiration:
- Has the feeling of a mini D&D campaign run by a friend
- Card game format keeps it simple, no animation bloat
- Easy to play, story-rich

Constraints:
- Ship quickly
- Solid and fun
- Simple but engaging
- Mysterious storytelling (Dark Souls / Hyper Light Drifter): very little
  dialogue, lore through flavor text on cards and item descriptions
- 2 NPCs (small interactions, not full RPG quest chains)
- Possibly outskirt areas but no travel between full towns
- No huge backstories — keep scope tight

Design decisions made on 2026-06-15:
- Resolution: D&D-style skill checks (sum cards against difficulty class)
- Run structure: Roguelike (one run from $2 → mayor; permanent unlocks between)
- Deck: Deckbuilder (items/clothing/drinks add cards to your deck)
- Contested encounters: Opponent draws cards too (head-to-head)
- Scope: Lean MVP — Deadhand town + outskirts + 2 NPCs + tight content set
